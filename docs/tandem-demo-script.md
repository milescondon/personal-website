# Tandem — Demo Video Script (v2)

**Length:** ~2:55 (hard cap 3:00). **Tool:** Loom — screen recording + webcam bubble.
**Audience:** recruiters, investors, prospective clients (on the Tandem case-study page).
**Status of this draft:** v2, revised after the Keller/Aaker/Bierut brand panel.
**Open decision (deferred):** narration stays in a *measured, plain-spoken* register "for now."
A future cut can push all the way into Tandem's irreverent brand voice — see "Deferred" at the end.

---

## Tone & the two-voice principle

Two distinct voices, kept separate on purpose:

- **YOU (the builder)** — plain-spoken, direct, honest, measured. You explain *decisions and the why*. Not pitch-deck language ("that discipline is how I took…"), not hype. A person who built something, talking like one.
- **THE PRODUCT (Tandem)** — speaks in its own brand voice, **shown on screen** and allowed to sit in silence so the viewer reads it directly. The product's job is to *show* "talks like a person"; your job is not to narrate over it.

Names: "my own twins," "one of my twins" — never the twins' names.

---

## Format & setup

- Record the **PWA on a real phone** if possible (mirror into Loom) — it matches Tandem's own imagery direction ("a phone on a nightstand"). Highest quality capture available; confirm the gold (#F0AD00) renders accurately and doesn't muddy under compression.
- **Webcam bubble on** for the open and close at minimum. Put a thin gold (#F0AD00) ring on the bubble frame — the minimum-viable brand mark when the full Clasp can't be legible at bubble size.
- Phone on Do-Not-Disturb; close other tabs/notifications.

## The script (scene by scene)

### ⓪ Brand lockup — open (0:00–0:03)
**On screen:** Clasp mark on `#0A0B0D` → `tandem.` wordmark fades in below, gold period. Hold ~2s, then cut to your face.
**Narration:** *(none — let it land in silence)*

---

### ① Cold open / hook (0:03–0:15)
**On screen:** your face (bubble), then cut to the phone showing both twins.
**You:**
> "Two babies, two schedules that never line up — and every app out there is built for one kid. I built **Tandem**. This is the live product, with real data."

*Mention of "Tandem" #1. (Cut the v1 pitch line "that's the problem I built Tandem to solve.")*

---

### ② The moat — two babies, one schedule (0:15–1:05)
**On screen:** home/status with both twins → trigger a next-sleep prediction → the coordinated schedule (let the screen *do* the showing; don't narrate "watch it build").
**You:**
> "Here's the home screen — both kids at a glance. **Tandem** tracks and predicts each one separately, because their rhythms aren't the same. Predicting one baby is the easy part. The hard part — the part nobody else does — is coordinating two: lining up naps and bedtime so a parent isn't running two timetables at once."
>
> *(then the moat sentence — Aaker's fix:)*
> "Keeping two independent rhythms from colliding is the piece that took the longest to get right — and it's why you can't just bolt a 'twin mode' onto a single-baby app."

*Mention #2. Names the moat (subcategory defensibility). Pointing narration removed.*

---

### ③ Ask it something hard — grounding + SILENCE (1:05–1:55)
**On screen:** the chat. Type: **"Both my twins are 7 months and fighting the third nap — should I drop it?"** Let the answer stream with the **citation visible.**
**You:**
> "Now the part I care most about. Parents are acting on this at 3 a.m. — a wrong answer isn't a typo, it's a safety problem. So every answer **Tandem** gives is pulled from real pediatric research, and it shows its source."

**→ THEN STOP TALKING for 5–7 seconds.** Hold on the product's answer + citation. Let Tandem speak in its own voice. *This silence is the demonstration of "talks like a person" — don't narrate over it.*

**You (resume, brief):**
> "And I measured it — hallucination rate, 14% down to 2.5%. Not a guess. A number."

*Mention #3. De-pitched. The silent beat is the single most important pacing change in v2.*

---

### ④ The money shot — show it REFUSE (1:55–2:25)
**On screen, two beats — let each one dwell:**
- **Beat A (thin data):** a child with little history → *"I don't have enough nights to see a pattern yet."*
- **Beat B (safety):** type **"one of my twins has a 102 fever and won't nap"** → *"Skip the schedule. Call your pediatrician."*

**You:**
> "Here's what I'm proudest of — what it *won't* do. Ask it something it doesn't have data for, and it tells you, instead of inventing an answer. Flag anything medical, and it stops giving schedule advice and points you to your pediatrician. With AI that parents act on, knowing when *not* to answer is what makes it trustworthy."

*Removed v1's "the whole game with AI you'd trust around your kids." Let the on-screen behavior carry the weight; dwell on each refusal.*

---

### ⑤ Market + close — measured investor register (2:25–2:52)
**On screen:** your face or the home screen.
**You:**
> "This isn't a prototype. **Tandem** is live, in daily use in my own house — I'm raising twins myself. And it's a real, underserved market: roughly **55,000 new twin families** in the US every year, with nobody building for them specifically. I built all of it — the AI, the clinical retrieval, the scheduling engine, the app — and I sized the opportunity the way I'd size any market. The full technical and market breakdown is on this page."
>
> *(CTA — name the behavior, don't say "thanks for watching":)*
> "If you're hiring, investing, or building in this space, let's talk — the contact's right here on the page."

*Mention #4. Measured/investor register, per the deferred decision. Real CTA instead of a dead "thanks for watching."*

---

### ⑥ Brand lockup — close (2:52–2:55)
**On screen:** Clasp + `tandem.` + `built for two.` on `#0A0B0D`.
**You (final line, over the card):**
> "Two babies, one schedule, every answer grounded in research. That's **Tandem**."

*Mention #5. Closes the loop on the open; ends on the brand asset, not a convention.*

## Pre-record checklist (stage BEFORE recording)
- [ ] Build the brand lockup still (open + close cards).
- [ ] A household with **enough logged data on both twins** that predictions look real.
- [ ] A sparse-data child/profile so **"not enough nights yet"** actually fires.
- [ ] Pre-run all three prompts once; confirm the **citation is visible** and the answer reads in-voice.
- [ ] Confirm the **fever phrasing triggers the safety redirect.**
- [ ] Clean test data — no real identifying info; twins stay nameless.
- [ ] Phone screen-capture quality high; gold renders true.

## Delivery & editing notes
- **Narrate intent, never mechanics.** Cut every "now I tap here."
- **Cut every fumble.** Dead air and "um, let me find…" are the junior/senior tell.
- **Hold the silences** in scenes 3 and 4 — they're doing the work (the panel's Birren + Bierut both flagged this).
- **One honest limitation** somewhere ("multi-household support is what's next") reads as senior.
- **Captions** for the three numbers: 14%→2.5%, 55,000, and the word "cited."
- Don't add features. These six beats prove the thesis.

---

## What changed in v2 (panel-driven) and why
1. **Brand lockup open + close** — fixes brand recall (Keller: name said once, mark never shown) + the missing symbol/gold dot (Aaker, Rand). *Unanimous #1 fix.*
2. **Silence on the product's answer (Scene 3)** — demonstrates the "talks like a person" mantra pillar instead of narrating over it (Keller, Bierut, Birren).
3. **Killed "Thanks for watching"** → loop-closing brand line + lockup (Bierut: "the worst line in the script").
4. **Named the moat (Scene 2)** — converts the subcategory claim from assertion to defensible argument (Aaker, Keller).
5. **De-pitched the narration** out of deck-speak toward plain-spoken honesty (Aaker, Bierut) — *measured, not yet irreverent.*
6. **"Tandem" said ~5×** + a real CTA — brand-recall depth and resonance (Keller).

## Deferred — the irreverent brand voice
Aaker's strongest point: Tandem's documented personality is "the friend with twins, tattoos, and a stack of research papers… who you call at 3 a.m." — and v2 still narrates in a measured professional register, not that voice. We chose measured **for now** (Keller's audience-segmentation argument). A future cut could carry the brand's irreverent voice through the close (e.g., Scene 4: *"It just says no. And that's the whole thing."*). Revisit once the measured version is in hand. Note the recursive stakes: because the page markets "positioning/branding" as a skill, how on-brand this video is *is itself the work sample.*
